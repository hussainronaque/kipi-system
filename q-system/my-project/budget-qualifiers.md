# Job Opportunity Qualifiers

> Adapted for job hunting. "Budget" = company can/will hire at Hussain's level with real comp.

## Keep Signals (worth applying / reaching out)

- Salary or compensation range listed — especially USD
- Title explicitly says Junior, Associate, Entry-Level, or Intern
- Stack mentions Node.js, Python, Django, React, PostgreSQL, Docker, Azure, or AWS
- Remote-first or fully remote (not "remote optional")
- Company stage: bootstrapped product, seed, or Series A/B startup
- Team size under 50 engineers (more room to grow, less bureaucracy)
- Product company — not agency or consulting firm
- Async-friendly, results-oriented, or output-focused culture mentioned
- Pakistan not excluded from remote eligibility
- YC, Techstars, or known accelerator backed = legitimacy signal
- Active engineering blog = team is growing and building

## Skip Signals (not worth pursuing)

- "5+ years experience required" on a junior title — mismatch, skip
- No comp range + not fully remote = likely below-market PKR role
- "Rockstar", "ninja", "10x developer" language — bad culture signal
- Pakistan explicitly excluded from remote roles
- Equity-only or "competitive salary" with zero numbers — avoid
- Agency or outsourcing firm (client work, no product ownership)
- Role requires active security clearance
- "Must be available 9–5 EST / PST" with no flexibility — PKT overlap is hard
- Job posted 30+ days ago with no applicant movement — likely filled or frozen

## Notes

- For remote USD roles: comp range matters more than company prestige
- For onsite Pakistan: comp in PKR is fine, but look for product ownership
- Research publication background = edge for AI/ML roles — weight these higher
- Azure/APIM experience = rare at junior level — highlight for cloud/platform roles
